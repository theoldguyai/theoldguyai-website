Issue #001

Can AI Write a Sermon? Pastors Are Doing It — Here's What the Bible Says About It

Over 60% of pastors now use AI for sermon preparation. This week we look at what that means for authenticity, the Holy Spirit's role, and the difference between a tool and a crutch — from both sides of the conversation.

The Promise

More time for pastoral care. Better research. Broader Scripture context in minutes.

The Concern

Authenticity at risk. AI has no faith. Can a machine-assisted sermon carry the Spirit?

Pastor's Take

Issue #002

AI and the Image of God — What Does Genesis 1:27 Say About Creating Artificial Intelligence?

Theologians, philosophers, and AI researchers are all asking the same question from different angles: if humans are made in God's image and humans create AI — what does that make AI? This week we dig into the theology and the technology together.

The Promise

AI as an expression of human creativity — a reflection of the creative nature God gave us.

The Concern

Creating artificial "minds" raises deep questions about the uniqueness of human consciousness and the soul.

Pastor's Take

"We've always used tools. The question isn't whether we build AI — it's whether we remain humble enough to remember we are not God, even when we're building things that seem god-like."

Issue #003

AI Bible Study Tools — 3 That Help and 2 That Could Lead You Astray

Not all AI Bible tools are created equal. Some genuinely deepen your understanding of Scripture. Others produce confident-sounding answers that are theologically wrong. This week we test five tools and tell you exactly which ones to trust — and which ones to question.

The Promise

Instant cross-references, historical context, and original language insights available to everyone.

The Concern

AI hallucinates. It can produce doctrinally incorrect interpretations with complete confidence.

Pastor's Take
